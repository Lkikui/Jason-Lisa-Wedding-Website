Things To Do

Yosemite National Park Day Tour

Wander down winding trails laden with pine needles, gaze at torrents of cascading water falling from granite cliffs, and explore open meadows flanked by towering rock formations. Ride to Yosemite National Park and spend a day uncovering the stunning corners of this magical landscape.

Hop aboard convenient transportation from your hotel and cruise north, crossing over the Golden Gate Bridge and riding through rolling hills until you arrive at the gates of Yosemite. Disembark and follow your guide for a pleasant walk down scenic hiking trails. As you stroll, listen to fascinating commentary about the natural history of this 1,190-square-mile (3,082-sq-km) wonderland.

Look for rainbows in the mist of Bridalveil Falls, watch fearless climbers scale the rocky face of El Capitan and Half Dome, or simply sit and relax next to the swift currents of the Merced River. When you're ready for a break, make your way to Yosemite Village to check out intriguing galleries, do some souvenir shopping, or consider grabbing lunch with options ranging from quick-and-easy cafes to fine dining at the Ahwahnee Hotel.

No matter how you spend your time at Yosemite, you're sure to be met with incredible views, breathtaking landscapes, and countless photo opportunities before you meet back up with your ride and return to San Francisco.

Golden Gate Sky Tour

620 Airport Way #7, San Carlos, CA

Take an aerial tour in the skies above San Francisco, catching glimpses of the ochre spans of the Golden Gate Bridge, admiring bird's-eye views of boats crisscrossing the Bay, and looking for the city's most well-known landmarks from the window seat of a comfortable plane.

Just a short drive south from downtown, reach the runways at San Carlos Airport where you can meet up with your pilot and climb aboard the plane. Buckle up during the final safety check and then zoom down the tarmac to lift off into the sky.

Set a course along the coast above Highway 101, cruising directly over the city for stunning looks at the downtown skyline, including the checkered green grasses at AT&T Park, and the pointed top of the Transamerica Pyramid. Continue on to snap photos of the Golden Gate Bridge's massive towers and then bank to the east for a view of the formidable shores of Alcatraz Island and the charming streets of Sausalito's quaint waterfront.

Follow the coast back to the airport, making passes over the towns of Daly City and Pacifica before checking out the dramatic cliffs that define the shape of Half Moon Bay's breathtaking coastline. A quick ride over the Golden Gate National Recreation Area brings you back to San Carlos, where you can make a graceful landing before you head back to the city.

California's Great America

4701 Great America Pkwy, Santa Clara, CA 95054, USA

California's Great America is a 100-acre (40 ha) amusement park located in Santa Clara, California. Owned and operated by Cedar Fair, it originally opened as Marriott's Great America in 1976 as one of two parks built and operated by the Marriott Corporation. The park has appeared in 1994 films Beverly Hills Cop III and Getting Even with Dad and features over 40 rides and attractions. One of its most notable attractions, Gold Striker, has been featured as a top-ranked wooden roller coaster in Amusement Today's annual Golden Ticket Awards publication.

Municipal Rose Garden

Dana Ave & Naglee Ave, San Jose, CA 95112, USA

+1 (408) 794-7274

Rolling green lawns and colorful showy blooms highlight the San José Municipal Rose Garden. In fact, hardly a day passes when some species is not in full bloom, with more than 3,500 plantings and 189 varieties featured.

San José's 5 1/2–acre Municipal Rose Garden – a one-time prune orchard – is today one of the most attractive of its kind in the world, drawing thousands of visitors each year. The garden is exclusively devoted to shrubs of the rose family and features over 4,000 rose shrubs with 189 varieties represented. Hybrid–teas comprise 75% of the plantings. These shrubs are characterized by a single high centered bloom per stem.

Cathedral Basilica of St. Joseph

80 S Market St, San Jose, CA 95113, USA

The Cathedral Basilica of St. Joseph is a large Roman Catholic church located in Downtown San Jose. The minor basilica is the cathedral for the Roman Catholic Diocese of San Jose in California. The basilica is named for Saint Joseph, patron saint of the universal church.

The original St. Joseph's Church was built on the site of the current basilica in 1803, and was the first non-mission parish built in California. The original adobe structure was damaged by earthquakes in 1818 and 1822, and a new adobe church was built from 1835 to 1846. The second church was severely damaged by the 1868 Hayward earthquake, and work on the third church began in 1869. The third church was destroyed by fire in 1875, and a temporary fourth church was built a few blocks away while the fifth and current church was being constructed. The fifth church was dedicated by Joseph Alemany, Archbishop of San Francisco, in 1877 while construction continued. The current portico was completed in 1884, and the large dome was finished in 1885.

In 1981, a major renovation project was begun at the church, which was to become the cathedral for the new Roman Catholic Bishop of San Jose. In 1985, the church was elevated to a cathedral, pending completion of the restoration in 1990. It replaced Saint Patrick Proto-Cathedral Parish, located a few blocks away, as the cathedral of the diocese. The cathedral was made a minor basilica by Pope John Paul II in 1997.

The Cathedral Basilica of St. Joseph is listed as a California Historical Landmark and is listed on the National Register of Historic Places.

Plaza de César Chávez

1 Paseo De San Antonio, San Jose, CA 95113, USA

Plaza de César Chávez is a small, 2.2 acre (9,000 m²) park in Downtown San Jose, California, USA, named after César Chávez (originally "The Plaza", then "Pueblo Plaza") in 1993. It is surrounded by South Market Street, across which is The Tech Museum of Innovation, the San Jose Museum of Art, the Circle of Palms Plaza, the Fairmont Hotel and the Marriott San Jose Hotel. At the south end is a statue of the "Plumed Serpent" Quetzalcoatl.

Japanese Friendship Garden

1300 Senter Rd, San Jose, CA 95112, USA

This beautiful and tranquil garden is an expanse of peace in the middle of the city. The Japanese Friendship Garden (JFG) is a living symbol of the "Sister City" relationship between Okayama, Japan and San José. It serves as a popular setting for the perfect place for weddings and celebrations but it best serves San Jose residents as a place to sit and reflect on the beauty and power of nature.

Rosicrucian Egyptian Museum

1660 Park Ave, San Jose, CA 95191, USA

The Rosicrucian Egyptian Museum (REM) is devoted to Ancient Egypt, located at Rosicrucian Park in the Rose Garden neighborhood of San Jose, California, United States. It was founded by the Ancient Mystical Order Rosae Crucis. The Rosicrucian Order continues to support and expand the museum and its educational and scientific activities.
